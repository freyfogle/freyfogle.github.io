I'm an entrepreneur living the good life in Barcelona (Tres Torres/Sarrià) with my wife and two children.
My blog.

About me:

  • Currently my main project is learning the hands-on details of running a SaaS business. I'm co-founder of OpenCage Data, we provide an API to convert coordinates to places.
    It's a fun but challenging business that still gives me plenty of flexibility to spend time with my kids.
  • I'm an occasional angel investor (my AngelList profile), investing in early-stage startups I can help via my domain knowledge or network, or where I know the founders. I'm particularly interested in geo, API-based businesses, aggregation, classifieds, and "academia2.0".
    I'm an investor in: Cashwave, GetAgent, Hubtype, Huddlebuy, Overleaf, Showroom, SplashMaps, Storage Made Easy, and What3Words.
  • Before moving to Barcelona in 2015 I spent ten years in London where I co-founded Lokku Ltd. We built the real estate search engine Nestoria, which we eventually operated in nine different countries. Our customers were all the major media groups across those markets.
    In May 2015 Lokku was acquired by Mitula Group. It was a fun ride.
  • Since 2010 I've run #geomob, the London geo-innovation meetup. We meet 5 or 6 times per year, with talks from start-ups, global brands, academics, and hobbyists - anyone doing anything interesting around location.
  • I occasionally interview interesting people from the geo world on GeoHipster, where I myself was interviewed back in 2014.
  • My career started as the first software developer at Yahoo! Germany in Munich, way back in the early days of the internet. I was there for five years. Initially I worked on various content products; my final two years I was head of search and marketplace engineering for Europe. Good times, I learned a lot.
  • I grew up back and forth between the US and Germany. I'm not really "from" anywhere.
    The NY Times American dialect quiz says I speak English as if I'm from Santa Fe, New Mexico, which is bizzare as I've been there only once for a two day visit. Der Spiegel Sprachatlas behauptet ich würde so sprechen als ob ich aus Leinefelde käme, was aber nicht ganz zutrifft. Oft sind wir aber in Heiligenstadt, was nicht weit weg ist - meine Familie kommt da aus der Nähe. My Spanish is not yet magnífico, but gradually improving. My accent can probably best be described as "faltering tourist".
